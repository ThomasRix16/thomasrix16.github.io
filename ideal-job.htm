My Ideal Job

Emerging Technology Analyst – BHP

The role is responsible for developing BHP’s house view on technology trends that will have the largest impact on our markets and external environment. The ideal candidate will have a minimum of 3-5 years of experience with a demonstrable track record in analysis and forecasting of emerging trends and technologies.

BHP Careers Website

About the role:

This job appeals to me because it focuses on analysing new technologies to be utilized in industry. Taking activities and systems that are already in place and making them more efficient is something that all big businesses are currently looking to achieve.

For a company like BHP that has an endless stream of the most mundane tasks all the way through to some of the most complex, it would be fascinating to assist in helping to make these tasks more efficient. The large amount of corporate resources would certainly assist in making this job rather exciting.

Skills Required:

  • A high level of teamwork and research skills
  • An enthusiasm for technology and how it can make a business more efficient
  • Strong interpersonal skills and the ability to tap into a large network
  • The ability to seek answers to uncommon problems
  • The ability to translate technical jargon in to easier to understand language for all audiences
  • Advanced knowledge of the Microsoft suite especially Excel
  • A good working knowledge of data visualisation software, including Spotfire or Tableau.

My Current Skills:

  • Basic quantitative and qualitative research skills
  • Ability to work as part of a diverse team
  • Ability to be self-motivated
  • Intermediate Excel modeling capability along with the remainder of the Microsoft suite
  • Drive to learn about technologies and their supportive attributes in industry

Gaining the required skills will require more than just study, there is a certain amount of industry experience that needs to be gained. This could be through additional courses or attendance at industry events and conferences.
